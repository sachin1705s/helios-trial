## The Core Principle

Every piece of content must make one thing obvious: **you don't just watch the character, you talk to them.** The interactivity is the product. Content that shows a one-way interaction undersells the platform.

## C. Content Formats

### 1. Challenge Formats
- **"Make it break"** — Try to get the character out of character. Community submits best attempts.
- **"60 second pitch"** — Pitch your idea to a Shark. Character funds it or tears it apart.
- **"Convince me"** — Change a character's known opinion.
- **"Lie detector"** — Tell the character something false. Does it call you out?
- **"One question challenge"** — Community votes on the single best question to ask next.

### 2. Reaction Content *(easy daily production)*
- Character reacts to today's trending Twitter topic
- Character reacts to a user's resume / LinkedIn profile
- Character reacts to IPL scorecard after a loss
- Character reacts to GenZ slang ("explain 'rizz' to Manmohan Singh")
- Character reacts to a startup pitch

### 3. Roleplay Scenarios
- Job interview with a famous villain
- "Your mom is [character]" — they check in on you
- Therapy session with a Bollywood character known for terrible life choices
- Performance review from your favourite movie boss
- First date with a historical figure

### 4. Education Wrapped in Entertainment
- Shark explains a business concept using a real company example
- "Explain [topic] like you're [character]" — Gabbar explains inflation, Mowgli explains climate change
- UPSC characters quiz you live — get graded
- Historical figure reacts to modern news ("Chanakya reads today's Parliament session")

### 5. Emotional / Personal *(high save rate)*
- "Talk to a version of someone you miss"
- "What would [character] say about your year so far"
- "Vent to [character] about your worst week" — people share the response
- "Your childhood hero responds to who you've become"

### 6. Building in Public *(LinkedIn + X)*
- "We gave [character] a personality — here's what happened on day 1"
- Failed interactions — when the AI says something wrong. Raw, funny, honest.
- "1000 people talked to this character. Here's what they asked." — data as content
- Founder talks to the character live on camera

### 7. UGC Triggers *(audience makes the content)*
- "What's the most unhinged question you'd ask [character]?" — replies become content queue
- "Submit your 60-second pitch — we'll put it to the Shark" — post the result
- "Tell us which character to add next" — poll + audience ownership

### 8. Recurring Series *(builds habit)*
- **Monday character drop** — new character every week, same reveal format
- **"This week in [character]'s world"** — funniest interactions of the week
- **Versus** — two characters answer the same question, community votes
- **Character of the month** — multiple pieces + ends with a live Broadcast session

### 9. Completely Absurdist *(low effort, high ceiling)*
- "I let Gabbar Singh be my therapist for a week"
- "I used a Shark Tank shark to negotiate my salary raise"
- "I asked Chanakya to plan my monthly budget"
- "I made Shaktimaan explain NFTs"
- "I had two characters argue about pineapple on pizza"

## D. Party Games

All games run on Broadcast mode (multiplayer). Several work in single-stream with people gathered around.

### Core Games

**Hot Seat / Interrogation**
One player sits in the hot seat. Character (detective, judge) interrogates them about a crime the group invented. Player must answer in character. Group votes: guilty or not.
Best character: Sherlock Holmes, Gabbar Singh as don

**Two Truths One Lie — AI Edition**
Player tells the character two truths and one lie. Character decides which is the lie. Group bets before the reveal. Character explains its reasoning — often funnier than the answer.

**Pitch Wars**
Each player has 60 seconds to pitch the most ridiculous startup idea to a Shark. Character funds or rejects with reasoning. "An app that delivers excuses to your mom" pitched to Aman Gupta is the format.

**Roast Bracket**
Players submit a one-line description of another player (anonymously). Character roasts each player. Group rates each roast 1–10.

**Character Impersonation Challenge**
Player tries to convince the AI character that they are a different famous character. Points for every response where the character doesn't break the illusion.

**Lie Detector**
Players make bold statements. Character probes with follow-up questions, delivers verdict. Group bets on verdict before it comes.

**Dare the Character**
Players collectively dare the character to say something specific. First to get the target phrase wins the round.

**Drunk History**
Player explains a historical event as badly/dramatically as possible. Character reacts with increasing horror and corrects them. Points for most dramatic corrections.

**Story Circle**
Player 1 starts a story. Character continues it. Player 2 adds a sentence. Character continues. Gets increasingly unhinged. Audience votes on direction in Broadcast mode.

**Speed Round Trivia**
Character fires rapid-fire questions at each player. 10 questions, 10 seconds each. Character keeps score in character — Gabbar's trivia is intimidating, Sherlock's is condescending.

### Games Tied to Experiments

| Experiment | Game |
|-----------|------|
| Object Detection | Scavenger Hunt — character calls an object, first to hold it up wins |
| Gesture Mode | Simon Says — character calls gestures, miss three = eliminated |
| Drawing to Live | Pictionary reversed — character guesses what you drew |
| Custom Characters | Impersonation Reveal — guess who the custom character is based on how it talks |

## E. Micro Drama / Serialised Content

Short serialised episodes (60–90 seconds), cliffhangers, high emotional stakes. The drama is genuine because the AI's response is unpredictable.

**What makes it different:** Normal micro dramas are scripted. These are live interactions — no two episodes are identical, the audience knows it's real.

### Story Structures

**The Ongoing Investigation**
You are solving something. The character knows more than they're letting on. Each episode = one more piece. Ends with "There's one more thing you should know—"

**The Reluctant Mentor**
You are a student. The legendary character doesn't want to teach you. Each episode you prove yourself more. The payoff episode: character finally says "you might be ready."

**The Trial**
You (or a fictional character) are on trial. Each episode = one piece of evidence. Verdict episode is the finale. Audience sides with you or against you.

**The Relationship Arc**
Building a relationship with a character across episodes. Cold start → trust → conflict → resolution.
- Ep 1: Cold rejection
- Ep 3: Character tests you
- Ep 5: Pass/fail the test
- Ep 10: Payoff

**Confession Arc**
One secret. Multiple characters react to it across episodes. Each gives a completely different perspective.

### Series Ideas Ready to Shoot

- "30 Days to Convince Chanakya I Deserve Power" — daily episode, audience votes on what argument to try
- "I'm Building a Startup and Aman Gupta Won't Let Me Quit" — real stakes, weekly progress
- "Sandeep Bhaiya Grades My Life Choices" — emotional, one major life decision per episode
- "I'm Trying to Get Hired by Gabbar Singh" — pure comedy, HR nightmare format
- "The Trial of [Controversial Opinion]" — 5 episodes, different character-judges, audience votes on verdict

**The unique hook:** "I didn't script this. This is what actually happened."

## F. Murder Mystery / Investigation Format

**The most powerful format in this document.**

### Core Mechanic

```
Creator runs the story as episodes (content)
         ↓
Community interrogates suspects on the platform (engagement)
         ↓
Different people get different answers (virality)
         ↓
Community pools clues to solve it (retention)
         ↓
Creator reveals verdict using community evidence (payoff)
```

The mystery can't be solved by watching. You have to play.

### How It Works

- A crime occurs. Multiple characters are suspects. Each is live on the platform.
- Each suspect has: an alibi, something they're hiding, one clue revealed only with the right question, one contradiction with another suspect's story
- **The slip mechanic:** Push suspects hard enough and they contradict themselves. Community screenshots = organic viral moments
- **The trigger question:** "Ask them about the 11pm call" becomes its own piece of content

### Episode Structure

| Episode | Creator does | Community does |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Ep 1 | Introduces crime + suspects | Starts interrogating, posts findings |
| Ep 2 | Interrogates Suspect A on camera | "They told me something different" comments flood in |
| Ep 3 | Interrogates Suspect B, reveals contradiction | Community forms theories |
| Ep 4 | Brings in a community-found clue | Race to find more |
| Ep 5 | Verdict using community evidence | Payoff for everyone who participated |

### Story Ideas

**"What Really Happened to Sandeep Bhaiya"**
His death in Season 3 left questions. Characters from the show each have a version of events. Tag TVF, Sunny Hinduja. Emotional investment already exists.

**"The Startup Betrayal"**
A co-founder was pushed out the night before a ₹10 crore funding round. Four suspects: co-founders, lead investor, early employee.
Target: Startup Twitter, LinkedIn.

**"The IPL Fix"**
A match result was allegedly predetermined. Suspects: underperforming player, team owner, commentator who knew in advance, mysterious fixer.
Target: Cricket community during IPL.

**"The Boardroom Murder"**
Shark Tank setting. A deal was signed, then the founder died. Each shark had motive.

### The Platform Funnel

```
Watch episode → Want to solve it → Go to platform → Interrogate suspect
→ Find something new → Post about it → Drives more viewers
→ Viewers interrogate too → Cycle repeats
```

Every episode is an on-ramp. The mystery makes the platform feel essential, not optional.

## L. Measurement

Optimize for saves + shares (not likes). Track:

| Metric | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Saves | Real perceived value — people want to return |
| Shares | Distribution signal — does it travel beyond your audience |
| Profile visits | Does content make people curious about the product |
| Comment quality | Are people tagging friends? Tagging = viral signal |
| Time to first interaction | How long from landing page to talking to a character |
| Session length | One exchange or extended conversation |

Two tests per format. If neither pulls saves + shares → cut. If one does → double down immediately.
